My ERA

When I created my business, ERA, I didn’t realize how much that name would come to define different parts of my life.

Each heart represents one of those eras: a period of beginning, growth, and becoming.

The hearts are black because when I came into Tech Moms, life felt black and white. I didn’t know exactly what I wanted, only that I needed change. I wanted knowledge, direction, and a reason to feel proud again.

1st Heart: The Beginning of ERA

This was when I first started ERA & Co. At the time, I was working full-time at Poplin, a remote job that gave me flexibility but drained me emotionally. I was learning how to run a business while trying to stay afloat in a job that made me feel small.

Through ERA, I taught myself how to register an LLC, design packaging, create incense and candles, and build a Shopify website. I learned how hard it is to get people to care about your product. I made a few sales, but eventually I had to pause. My mental health was slipping, and I needed to find motivation again.

2nd Heart: The Growth of ERA

After months of burnout, I started going to Pilates every morning. Four months in, my energy came back. That’s when my mom told me about Tech Moms.

I joined because I needed confidence. I wanted to learn what I was capable of, not just what my job defined me as. I had no idea what HTML, CSS, or JavaScript even meant, but I liked that. Growth starts with being uncomfortable.

During Tech Moms, I began restructuring my career goals. I rebuilt my resume and started applying for federal jobs. I applied to the IRS and later to a legal position in the federal government.

That interview changed everything. It wasn’t with HR like I was used to, but with two government lawyers, including the Deputy Chief Counsel. I was terrified but proud. The next day, Poplin announced layoffs and I was told my last day would be December 19. I felt there was a reason for the timing.

Two weeks later, the lawyer called to say I didn’t get the original job, but he created a new position just for me. A week later, he told me I was selected. I’m now waiting on my background check and drug test to start this new chapter.

3rd Heart: The Becoming of ERA

This heart represents resilience, learning to trust timing, intuition, and my own worth.

ERA is not just a business name anymore. It is a reminder that I am always in an era of becoming, learning, rebuilding, and finding purpose through every challenge.
